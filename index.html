About

I’m Obaid Raza, a builder at heart. My journey began with solving problems—whether it was keeping Loyola University’s IT systems running or competing in hackathons and CTFs where every second mattered.

Along the way, I found my passion at the crossroads of AI, cybersecurity, and product development.

I’ve built things that learn—like transformer-based NLP pipelines and GAN-powered chatbots. I’ve also built things that defend—intrusion detection systems tuned with Snort, Wireshark, and ELK that make networks smarter and safer.

For me, technology isn’t just about code; it’s about creating systems that people can trust and rely on.

Outside of projects, I thrive in communities. From contributing to Google Developer Groups to collaborating in Loyola’s AI Club, or trading insights at Y Combinator Startup School. I believe the best ideas grow when shared.

My philosophy is simple: build what matters, and build it securely. I aim to push the boundaries of AI while ensuring the systems we create are resilient, ethical, and human-centered.

Right now, I’m seeking opportunities to join a forward-thinking team where I can design, secure, and scale technology that makes a real impact.
